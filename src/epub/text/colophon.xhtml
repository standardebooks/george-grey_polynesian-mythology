Colophon

Polynesian Mythology
was published in 1855 by
George Grey.

This ebook was produced for
Standard Ebooks
by
Asher Smith,
and is based on a transcription produced in 2005 by
AEL Data
for the
New Zealand Electronic Text Collection
and on digital scans from the
Internet Archive.

The cover page is adapted from
Tāwhiao Matutaera Pōtatau Te Wherowhero,
a painting completed in 1882 by
Gottfried Lindauer.
Tāwhiao was the paramount chief of the Waikato Tainui tribes; he reigned as the second Māori king between 1860 and his death in 1894, and led a failed delegation to England to petition Queen Victoria about the dishonoured Treaty of Waitangi.
